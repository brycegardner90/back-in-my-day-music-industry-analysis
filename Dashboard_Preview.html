Incumbency Persistence by Decade

Fewer new artists are breaking into the Top 10 each year (43.4 → 31.6 average), while the artists already there are more likely to still be there the following week (81.9% → 86.9% persistence). Read together, that's the winner-take-all signature the project set out to test: once an artist breaks through, the current era's algorithmic curation seems to keep them there longer than radio-DJ-era gatekeeping did. Correlational, not causal — see the pipeline plan doc for the filter-bubble research this pattern is consistent with.

1991 (BDS/SoundScan) and 2013 (streaming-equivalency) reference lines mark measurement regime changes on the year chart above — dips/jumps at those points are partly measurement artifacts, not pure discovery-pathway shifts.
